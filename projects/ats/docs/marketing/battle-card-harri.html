Battle Card: Harri

Competitor: Harri (harri.com) — Enterprise hospitality WFM platform with integrated ATS

Target segment: Multi-location hospitality groups, 100+ staff, enterprise budget

Geography: US, UK, Europe (54+ countries supported)

Pricing: ~$875/month for 100 employees across 10 locations (Employee Experience tier); custom/enterprise; no free plan

Overview

Harri is the most feature-complete hospitality-native workforce management platform in the market. It covers the full employee lifecycle: recruitment, scheduling, time tracking, communication, HR, and payroll (UK only). The ATS module is embedded within the broader platform rather than sold standalone. Harri is the strongest direct competitor for multi-location pipeline visibility.

When Harri Wins

  • Enterprise scale: 500+ location chains with dedicated TA teams and enterprise budgets
  • Integrated WFM suite: Operators who need recruitment, scheduling, time tracking, and payroll in one platform
  • Forecast-based scheduling: Labour forecasting tightly coupled with headcount planning is a unique strength
  • Existing Harri contract: If the operator is satisfied and the contract is not up for renewal, switching cost is high

When Lapam-ATS Wins

  • Mid-market pricing constraint: Operators with £300–600/month budget who need multi-location visibility but can't justify $875+/month
  • Seasonal operations: Coastal hotels, ski resorts, event venues — any operator who re-hires the same population year after year. Harri has no seasonal CRM.
  • Global payroll flexibility: Harri payroll is UK-only. Operators in the US, EU, or multi-jurisdiction need external payroll anyway. Lapam-ATS is payroll-agnostic by design.
  • Mobile-first manager adoption: Operators who have tried Harri and watched managers revert to WhatsApp because the 5-app experience was too fragmented
  • Standalone ATS need: Operators who already use Deputy, 7shifts, Sage, or Xero and just need an ATS that hands off cleanly — not a bundled platform
Key positioning message: "The multi-location visibility and compliance depth of Harri — at a price that doesn't require board approval."

Proof Points

Pricing

Harri starts at approximately $875/month for 100 employees across 10 locations (Connecteam review, Jan 2026). Lapam-ATS targets £300–600/month for 5–50 locations. For a 10-location mid-market group, that's a 60–70% cost reduction.

Source: Third-party review data (Connecteam, Jan 2026); Lapam-ATS positioning framework

Seasonal CRM Gap

Claire persona (6 coastal properties, sole HR): "I spent three weeks last March manually ringing through a spreadsheet of 200 people I hired two years ago to find out who was available. Two of them had moved. Forty didn't answer. Twenty said yes." Operators pay 15–25% agency markup for peak season coverage. A CRM that substitutes this is ROI-positive in the first season.

Source: ICP-and-personas.md; agency spend displacement analysis

Mobile Fragmentation

Harri has 5 separate mobile apps (Harri Live: 4.7–4.8 stars on app stores, but fragmented). G2 reviews note: "Glitchy at times, which may hinder the overall experience" and "5 separate mobile apps — fragmented." Lapam-ATS is a single mobile-optimised PWA.

Source: Competitors.md; G2/Capterra user reviews

UK Payroll Limitation

Harri payroll module is UK-only — not available for US or EU deployments. Global and US operators cannot run payroll natively within Harri. Very limited third-party payroll integrations even in US market.

Source: Competitors.md; Harri product documentation

Objection Handling

Objection: "We already use Harri and it works for us."

Response: "If Harri is working for you at the price you're paying, you should stay with them. We built Lapam-ATS for operators who need multi-location visibility and compliance depth but don't have an enterprise budget or don't need the full WFM platform. The operators who switch to us from Harri usually tell us two things: one, the price was a constraint — they couldn't justify $875+/month to the board; and two, they didn't need scheduling and payroll in one platform because they already had Deputy or 7shifts. If cost isn't a constraint and you're using Harri's full WFM suite, stay with them. If you're paying for features you don't use, we'd love to show you what we've built."

Objection: "Harri has scheduling integration — you don't."

Response: "You're right — Harri's scheduling integration is native and tightly coupled. If you need scheduling and recruitment in one platform, Harri is the right choice. But if you already use Deputy, 7shifts, or another WFM tool and you're happy with it, Lapam-ATS hands off cleanly to those systems. We're ATS-only. We don't try to replace your scheduling tool — we complement it. WFM handoff integration is on our roadmap for 3 months post-launch."
