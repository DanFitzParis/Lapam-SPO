# **Problem**

---

## The Informal Stack Fails at Scale

Hospitality groups operating 5–50 locations hire 200–2,000 people per year using:

- **Job board subscriptions** (Indeed, Hospitality Online)
- **Spreadsheets** (maintained by no one)
- **Email** (applications lost in inboxes)
- **WhatsApp groups** (managers texting CVs)

**Three failure modes:**

1. **No visibility** across locations — staffing crises discovered reactively
2. **Speed failure** — candidates take another job within hours if no response
3. **Compliance risk** — GDPR consent not captured, right-to-work checks on paper

<!-- Speaker notes: The informal stack is not a workflow problem — it is a structural ceiling. It works for 1–3 locations. At 10+ locations it collapses. -->

---

## What It Costs Them

**Turnover reality:**
- 70–80% annual turnover in hospitality (US BLS — highest of any sector)
- Every departure costs >£5,000 (recruiting, onboarding, lost productivity)

**For a 20-location group:**
- 30 staff per location × 70% turnover = 420 hires per year
- 420 × £5,000 = **£2.1M in turnover-related cost annually**

**An ATS that reduces cost-per-hire by 20% saves £420K per year.**

ROI case is immediate. The problem is price-exclusivity of current solutions.

# **Solution**

---

## Lapam-ATS: What It Is

A **standalone, multi-tenant SaaS ATS** built specifically for mid-market international hospitality groups.

**Not:**
- ❌ An enterprise WFM platform (we're ATS-only)
- ❌ A US-native tool with EU aspirations (UK/EU compliance from day one)
- ❌ A single-site tool (built for multi-location from the ground up)

**Core value proposition:**
Multi-location pipeline visibility, seasonal talent pool management, and compliance depth — at pricing that doesn't require board approval (£300–600/month for 5–50 locations).

---

## How It Works (User Flow)

**01 → Manager posts a job**
- Select role template (server, chef, housekeeper, bartender)
- AI drafts job description in 10 seconds
- Publishes to Indeed + Google for Jobs
- QR code + unique link generated

**02 → Candidate applies on mobile**
- No login. No account creation.
- 3-minute application, 4 screens
- GDPR consent captured inline
- Automated acknowledgement sent within 2 minutes

**03 → Manager reviews pipeline**
- Multi-location dashboard: all sites, one screen
- Candidate cards in Kanban stages: Applied → Screening → Interview → Offer → Hired
- Visual alerts on stalled pipelines

**04 → UK right-to-work verification**
- Candidate receives mobile link (no login)
- Submits share code or document via GBG IDVT integration
- Pass/fail result returned; timestamped audit record created

**05 → Digital offer, onboarding, handoff**
- Offer letter sent on mobile, accepted with a tap
- New hire syncs to Deputy/7shifts (WFM handoff)
- Payroll data ready for Sage/Xero

| Feature | Customer Benefit |

| **Multi-location pipeline dashboard** | Know which locations have staffing crises before they become service failures — one screen, real-time |

| **GDPR compliance infrastructure** | Consent captured at application, automated retention/deletion, audit-ready data subject access |

---

## Differentiators (What Competitors Don't Have)

### 1. Seasonal Talent Pool CRM
**The gap:** No ATS in the hospitality segment owns this feature.
- Eploy (UK-only) has partial seasonal CRM
- Harri, Hireology, Workstream, StaffedUp — none have talent pool re-engagement

**Why it wins:** Operators pay 15–25% agency markup for peak season coverage. A CRM that lets them re-hire 60% of last summer's roster from their own database substitutes agency spend. ROI-positive in the first season.

### 2. UK/EU Compliance Architecture
**The gap:** Every US-native competitor lacks meaningful EU/UK compliance investment.
- Workstream, Fountain, Hireology, StaffedUp, HigherMe, CareerPlug are all US-primary
- They are geography-limited by regulatory gap, not by choice

**Why it wins:** GDPR consent lifecycle, UK right-to-work workflow, and EU AI Act-ready screening are structural barriers. This is a compliance moat — expensive for US-native players to replicate.
