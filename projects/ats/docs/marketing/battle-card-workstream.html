Battle Card: Workstream

Competitor: Workstream (workstream.us) — Mobile-first hourly hiring platform (ATS + HR + payroll)

Target segment: Restaurant chains, retail, hourly multi-location operators (US-primary)

Geography: US-primary

Pricing: Essential: $109/location/month | Professional: $199/location/month | Enterprise: $399/location/month (transparent location-based pricing)

Overview

Workstream was built specifically for the speed problem in hourly hiring. Founded 2016 in San Francisco. Core thesis: hospitality/retail managers lose candidates because the process is too slow and too desktop-centric. Applications complete in under 3 minutes on mobile. Time-to-hire typically 3–5 days vs 2–3 weeks with traditional ATS. Notable clients: White Castle, Panda Express, Planet Fitness. Workstream is the gold standard for mobile-first hourly candidate engagement in the US market — but it is US-only.

When Workstream Wins

  • US-based operators: Single-country US operators who don't need EU/UK compliance and want the most proven mobile-first hiring platform in the market
  • Speed above all else: Operators who prioritise 3–5 day time-to-hire over analytics, compliance depth, or multi-location oversight
  • Text-first communication requirement: SMS is the dominant communication channel in the US; Workstream is optimised for this
  • Simplicity over feature breadth: Operators who want a narrow, fast tool and don't need seasonal CRM, advanced analytics, or complex compliance

When Lapam-ATS Wins

  • UK/EU operators: Any operator hiring across the UK, Ireland, France, Germany, Spain, or multi-jurisdiction EU. Workstream does not have GDPR or UK right-to-work compliance.
  • Multi-location oversight requirement: Operators managing 10+ locations who need a real-time dashboard showing all hiring activity on one screen. Workstream's multi-location reporting is basic.
  • Seasonal operations: Coastal hotels, ski resorts, event venues — any operator who re-hires the same population year after year. Workstream has no seasonal CRM.
  • Analytics & data-driven hiring: Operators who need time-to-fill, cost-per-hire, and source attribution by location and role. Workstream's analytics are limited.
  • WhatsApp communication: EU/UK operators need WhatsApp, not just SMS. Workstream is SMS-only. Lapam-ATS has WhatsApp on roadmap (6 months post-launch).
Key positioning message: "The simplicity and speed of Workstream — built for operators hiring across the UK and Europe, not just the US."

Objection Handling

Objection: "Workstream is proven. You're zero-to-one. Why would we take the risk?"

Response: "That's a fair question — and if you're a US-based operator, Workstream is probably the safer choice. It's a mature product with a proven track record. But if you're operating in the UK, Ireland, or the EU, Workstream isn't an option. They don't have GDPR compliance, they don't have UK right-to-work verification, and they don't serve international operators. We're not trying to out-feature Workstream in the US. We're solving the compliance and multi-location visibility gaps they haven't addressed."

Objection: "Workstream's pricing is competitive. Why pay more for Lapam-ATS?"

Response: "Workstream's Essential tier is $109/location/month; Lapam-ATS is £50–60/location/month (roughly $60–75 USD). We're not more expensive — we're comparable or cheaper. The difference is what you get for that price: Workstream gives you speed and simplicity in the US. Lapam-ATS gives you speed, simplicity, multi-location visibility, seasonal CRM, and UK/EU compliance. If you're a US-only operator and you don't need those features, Workstream is a great choice. But if you're hiring across the UK and EU, we're the only option that delivers both speed and compliance."

Objection: "What if we expand to the US? Will you support us?"

Response: "US market launch is on our roadmap for 12 months post-launch (Q2 2027). We'll add I-9/E-Verify compliance and EEOC reporting when we enter the US. If you're planning US expansion in the next 6 months and you need a tool that works in both markets today, you'll need to choose between us (UK/EU) and Workstream (US). But if your core operations are UK/EU and US expansion is 12+ months out, we'll be ready when you are."
