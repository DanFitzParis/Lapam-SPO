# Objection Handling Guide: Lapam-ATS

**Purpose:** Structured responses to common objections from prospective customers. Each objection includes context, recommended response, proof point, and bridge to a Lapam-ATS strength.

---

## Objection 1: "We already use [Harri / Hireology / Fourth]"

### Context
This comes up when the prospect is already using an enterprise ATS and has some level of satisfaction with it. Often raised by Group People Directors or COOs who have an existing contract. The objection may be genuine satisfaction or switching cost aversion.

### Response
*"You're in good company — those are excellent enterprise tools, and if they're working for you at the price you're paying, you should stay with them. We built Lapam-ATS for operators who need multi-location visibility and compliance depth but don't have an enterprise budget or don't need the full WFM platform that comes with it."*

*"The operators who switch to us from Harri usually tell us two things: one, the price was a constraint — they were spending $875+ per month and couldn't justify it to the board; and two, they didn't need scheduling, time and attendance, and payroll in one platform because they already had Deputy or 7shifts. They just needed an ATS that handed off cleanly to their existing tools."*

*"If cost isn't a constraint and you're using Harri's full WFM suite, stay with them. If you're paying for features you don't use, or if the contract is up for renewal and you're looking for alternatives, we'd love to show you what we've built."*

### Proof Point
Harri starts at approximately $875/month for 100 employees across 10 locations (Connecteam review, Jan 2026). Lapam-ATS targets £300–600/month for 5–50 locations. For a 10-location mid-market group, that's a **60–70% cost reduction** for comparable multi-location pipeline visibility.

### Bridge to Strength
**Lapam-ATS strength:** Seasonal talent pool CRM — a feature Harri does not have. *"Even if you stay with Harri for now, ask them about seasonal talent pool re-engagement. If they can't show you a CRM designed for re-hiring last season's workers, that's a gap we fill."*

## Objection 2: "We're too small for an ATS"

### Context
This usually comes from operators managing 3–8 locations who think "ATS" means "enterprise software for 500+ location chains." Often raised by owner-operators or GMs who have never used an ATS before and assume it's complex and burdensome.

### Response
*"I hear that a lot — and it's fair if you're thinking about Harri or Fourth, which are built for enterprise chains. But if you're hiring across 3, 5, or 8 locations, you're not too small — you're exactly the operator we built this for."*

*"The operators who say they're too small are usually the ones losing candidates because they're responding in 3 days instead of 3 hours, or they're manually ringing through last year's workers every spring, or they're discovering a staffing crisis when a manager calls them on Saturday night. The question isn't whether you're big enough for an ATS. The question is: what's the cost of not having visibility?"*

*"Lapam-ATS is priced for mid-market groups — £50–60 per location per month. For 5 locations, that's £250–300/month. If you're hiring 100–200 people per year across those locations, and each departure costs you >£5,000 in recruiting and onboarding, the tool pays for itself in 5–10 faster hires. If it doesn't, you shouldn't buy it."*

### Proof Point
A 20-location group with 70% annual turnover hires ~420 people per year at >£2.1M in turnover-related cost (research: OysterLink 2025, citing Fountain data). A tool that reduces cost-per-hire by 20% saves £420K annually. The ROI case scales down proportionally: a 5-location group hiring 100 people per year at £5K cost per hire spends £500K annually on turnover; a 20% reduction saves £100K.

### Bridge to Strength
**Lapam-ATS strength:** Self-service onboarding in under 30 minutes. *"You can have your first job live within 30 minutes of signing up. No demo required. No training webinar. No implementation project. If it's too complex or takes too long, you cancel. But we've designed it so your managers can use it if they can use WhatsApp."*

## Objection 3: "We can't afford another tool"

### Context
Budget objection. Common from operators managing tight margins, especially post-pandemic. May be genuine (no discretionary budget) or may be a negotiation tactic. Often raised by owner-operators or GMs who don't have budget authority above £500/month.

### Response
*"Fair. Let's talk numbers. A 20-location group with 70% turnover hires about 400 people per year. Each departure costs >£5,000 in recruiting, onboarding, and lost productivity. That's £2M in turnover-related cost every year."*

*"If Lapam-ATS reduces your time-to-fill by 2 weeks per hire — faster candidate engagement, no applications lost in email, proactive alerts on stalled pipelines — you're reducing the duration each role remains unfilled. Every unfilled role is lost revenue: reduced service capacity, overtime for remaining staff, worse reviews."*

*"Assume a conservative 10% reduction in total turnover cost from faster hiring. That's £200K in annual savings. Lapam-ATS costs £10,800 per year for 20 locations. The tool pays for itself 18 times over. If it doesn't save you money, you shouldn't buy it. But the ROI case is there."*

### Proof Point
Cost per departure: >£5,000 (OysterLink 2025, citing Fountain). Annual turnover in hospitality: 70–80% (US BLS — highest of any sector). For a 10-location group with 30 staff per location, that's 210 hires per year at £1.05M in turnover-related cost. A 10% reduction saves £105K. Lapam-ATS costs £6K per year (10 locations × £50/location/month × 12). ROI: 17.5x in year one.

### Bridge to Strength
**Lapam-ATS strength:** Pricing transparency and predictability. *"We price per location, not per user or per hire. You know exactly what it costs every month. No surprise fees. No sales-negotiated custom pricing. £50–60 per location per month. You can budget it."*

## Objection 4: "What about [Workstream / StaffedUp / HigherMe]? They're cheaper."

### Context
Competitor objection. Usually comes from operators who have done research and found US-native SMB tools with lower price points ($79–$149/month for single-site or small multi-site groups). Often raised by price-sensitive operators or those unfamiliar with EU/UK compliance requirements.

### Response
*"Great question — Workstream, StaffedUp, and HigherMe are all solid products for US operators. The challenge is: they're US-only. They don't have GDPR compliance infrastructure, they don't have UK right-to-work verification, and they don't serve international multi-location groups operating across the UK and EU."*

*"If you're a single-site operator in the US, Workstream is probably the right choice. But if you're operating across the UK and France, or the UK and Ireland, or 5+ locations in multiple jurisdictions, those tools aren't built for you. You need GDPR consent capture at application, automated retention and deletion, UK right-to-work workflow with GBG IDVT integration, and multi-jurisdiction compliance from day one. That's what we built."*

*"The reason they can price lower is because they're not investing in the compliance layer that UK and EU operators legally require. We are. That's the difference."*

### Proof Point
Workstream, StaffedUp, HigherMe, Fountain, CareerPlug — all US-primary with no documented EU/UK compliance investment (research: competitors.md). GDPR fines: up to 4% of global turnover. UK right-to-work fines: £45,000–£60,000 per illegal worker. Operators in the UK/EU cannot adopt a US-native tool without accepting material compliance risk.

### Bridge to Strength
**Lapam-ATS strength:** UK/EU compliance architecture as a structural moat. *"We're not just a UK localisation of a US product. GDPR consent lifecycle, UK right-to-work verification, EU AI Act-ready screening — these are built into the data model from day one. That's a 12-month compliance investment US-native tools haven't made and won't replicate quickly."*

## Objection 5: "We'll just build it ourselves"

### Context
Common from larger operators with internal engineering teams or operators who underestimate the complexity of building a multi-tenant SaaS ATS. Sometimes genuine (they have engineering capacity), sometimes a negotiation tactic, sometimes naive (they don't know what they don't know).

### Response
*"If you have engineering capacity to build a multi-tenant SaaS ATS with GDPR compliance, UK right-to-work integration, seasonal CRM, and multi-location pipeline dashboard, you probably should. But if you're a hospitality operator, not a software company, that's 12 months and £400K–£800K before you have a working product — and that's before ongoing maintenance, security patches, compliance updates, and feature development."*

*"The operators who say they'll build it themselves usually discover three things: one, they underestimated the compliance layer (GDPR consent lifecycle, UK right-to-work audit trail, EU AI Act requirements); two, they didn't budget for ongoing maintenance and regulatory updates; and three, their engineering team has better things to do than build HR software."*

*"We've already built it. We've already invested the £400K–£800K. We're maintaining it. We're handling the compliance updates. You get the product for £50–60 per location per month. The question is: do you want to be in the software business, or do you want to run your hospitality business?"*

### Proof Point
From ADR-001 tech stack (Lapam-ATS specifications): greenfield cloud-native monolith build estimate: £400K–£800K over 12 months, 4–6 engineers. Ongoing costs: cloud infrastructure (Vercel, Neon, Cloudflare R2), security monitoring, compliance audits, GBG IDVT API fees, regulatory updates (GDPR, UK RTW, EU AI Act). SaaS subscriptions: Clerk, Resend, Twilio, Anthropic. Annual run-rate post-launch: £80K–£150K before staffing.

### Bridge to Strength
**Lapam-ATS strength:** We've already made the compliance investment. *"The hardest part of building an ATS for the UK/EU market isn't the pipeline dashboard or the mobile application. It's the compliance layer: GDPR consent lifecycle, right-to-work audit trail, data deletion policies, EU AI Act human-in-the-loop requirements. We've already built that. You'd be starting from zero."*

## Objection 6: "We need [feature X] that you don't have yet"

### Context
Feature gap objection. Common features requested: WhatsApp candidate communication, video interview tooling, native iOS/Android app, multi-language UI, US I-9/E-Verify workflow. Often raised by prospects comparing Lapam-ATS feature list to Harri or Hireology.

### Response
*"Great feedback — and you're right, we don't have [feature X] at launch. We're launching with the core features that the research told us are purchase-level pain: multi-location visibility, seasonal talent pool CRM, UK right-to-work verification, and mobile-first no-login candidate application. [Feature X] is on our roadmap."*

**If the feature is on the roadmap (e.g., WhatsApp communication):**
*"WhatsApp candidate communication is roadmap for 6 months post-launch. We're currently in the Meta Business API verification process. SMS and email are live at launch; WhatsApp follows once verification is complete. If WhatsApp is a hard requirement for you to get started, we can put you on the early access list for that feature specifically and let you know when it's ready."*

**If the feature is explicitly excluded (e.g., video interview tooling):**
*"Video interview tooling is explicitly out of scope for us. The research didn't surface it as a primary pain across any of the personas we interviewed, and it adds significant third-party data processing surface that increases our compliance burden. If video interviews are critical to your hiring process, Harri or Hireology may be a better fit. We're focused on the features that solve the top 3 pains: visibility, speed, and compliance."*

### Proof Point
WhatsApp is the dominant mobile messaging platform in the EU/UK (research: SmartRecruiters Hospitality Recruitment Trends 2025). SMS response rates are lower than WhatsApp in European markets. Operators need WhatsApp communication to engage candidates effectively. Lapam-ATS roadmap: WhatsApp via 360dialog BSP integration, 6 months post-launch (Could-1 in PRD).

### Bridge to Strength
**Lapam-ATS strength:** Roadmap transparency and customer input. *"We're building in public. If [feature X] is critical for your hiring process, tell us. If we hear it from 10 operators, it moves up the roadmap. We're a zero-to-one product — we're still learning what matters most."*

## Objection 7: "Our managers won't use it — they'll just revert to WhatsApp"

### Context
Adoption risk objection. Common from Group People Directors or COOs who have tried to implement systems before and watched them fail because managers found them too burdensome. Often grounded in real experience (e.g., tried to implement Harri; managers stopped using it after 2 weeks).

### Response
*"That's a real risk — and it's the #1 reason ATS implementations fail in hospitality. If the tool feels like it was designed for a 200-person HR department and your GMs are running restaurants between services, they'll revert to WhatsApp within two weeks. We designed Lapam-ATS specifically to avoid this."*

*"Three decisions we made to keep managers engaged: one, mobile-first by design — post a job, review applications, schedule interviews, all from your phone. Two, no desktop steps — if it requires logging into an admin panel on a computer, it doesn't ship. Three, self-service onboarding in under 30 minutes — no training webinar, no implementation project. If your managers can use WhatsApp, they can use Lapam-ATS."*

*"The operators who've successfully rolled out ATS tools tell us the same thing: the GM's experience is the renewal risk. If they love it in the first two weeks, they'll keep using it. If they find it burdensome, they'll ghost the tool and you'll cancel the contract at renewal. We optimised for that first two weeks."*

### Proof Point
Marco persona (3-location restaurant GM, Netherlands): *"The system we tried before wanted me to log in, create an account, complete a 30-page onboarding — I'm a GM, not a HR manager. I never logged in again."* Harri user reviews (G2/Capterra): *"Steep learning curve; glitchy at times; 5 separate mobile apps — fragmented."*

### Bridge to Strength
**Lapam-ATS strength:** Mobile-first manager UX as a design constraint from day one. *"We tested the mobile UX with hospitality GMs before we built a single line of code. The rule was: if you can't post a job and review an application in under 10 minutes from your phone between services, it's not shipping. That's the design constraint."*

## Objection 8: "What if we grow past 50 locations? Will you scale with us?"

### Context
Future-proofing objection. Common from growth-stage operators or PE-backed groups planning aggressive expansion. Genuine concern: will Lapam-ATS become a constraint when they hit 100, 200, or 500 locations?

### Response
*"Great question — and it's the right one to ask. Lapam-ATS is built as a multi-tenant SaaS platform on a cloud-native stack (Next.js, Neon Postgres, Vercel edge functions). The architecture scales to 500+ locations without requiring a migration or platform change."*

*"The pricing model scales with you: £50–60 per location per month. At 50 locations, you're paying £2,500–£3,000/month. At 100 locations, you're paying £5,000–£6,000/month. We offer volume discounts at 50+ locations, and we move you to annual enterprise pricing with dedicated support at 100+ locations."*

*"The question isn't whether the platform can handle your growth — it can. The question is whether you need enterprise features we don't have yet: dedicated account management, custom integrations, white-label deployment, multi-brand support. If you do, Harri or Fourth may be the right choice at that scale. If you just need an ATS that works across 100 locations with the same features you're using at 20, we're the right fit."*

### Proof Point
Lapam-ATS technical architecture (ADR-001): cloud-native monolith on Vercel edge functions, Neon Postgres with connection pooling, Cloudflare R2 for document storage. Multi-tenancy enforced at application layer (tenantId on all data records). Scales horizontally with Vercel's edge network. No architectural ceiling at 500 locations.

### Bridge to Strength
**Lapam-ATS strength:** Pricing transparency at scale. *"Harri doesn't publish pricing. Fourth doesn't publish pricing. Hireology doesn't publish pricing. We do. You know what it costs at 10 locations, at 50 locations, at 100 locations. No sales-negotiated custom pricing. No surprise fees when you add location 51."*
