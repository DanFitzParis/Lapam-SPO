# Positioning Framework: Lapam-ATS

**Purpose:** One-page structured positioning document. Defines target customer, market category, pain point, promise, differentiators, and proof points.

---

## Target Customer

**Who this is for:**
- Independent hospitality groups operating 5–50 locations
- Operating across 2+ countries (UK, EU, or international)
- Hiring 200–2,000 people per year
- 1–3 HR generalists at group level; unit GMs handle day-to-day hiring
- Currently using informal stacks: job board subscriptions, spreadsheets, email, WhatsApp

**Segment size:**
- EU hospitality workforce: ~10.6M (Western/Northern/Southern Europe, Q1 2025)
- UK hospitality workforce: ~1.65M
- US leisure and hospitality: ~17M jobs
- Specific addressable market for 5–50 location multi-jurisdiction groups: not publicly quantified (research blocker)

**Geography:**
- Launch market: United Kingdom
- Phase 2: EU expansion (France, Germany, Spain, Netherlands, Ireland)
- Phase 3: US market (roadmap)

---

## Market Category

**Primary category:** Multi-location hospitality ATS

**Adjacent categories:**
- Workforce management (WFM) platforms with integrated ATS (Harri, Fourth)
- Multi-vertical ATS with hospitality segment (Hireology)
- US-native SMB hospitality ATS (Workstream, StaffedUp, HigherMe)

**How Lapam-ATS is positioned within the category:**
Lapam-ATS is a standalone ATS (not a bundled WFM platform) built specifically for mid-market international hospitality groups. It delivers the multi-location visibility and compliance depth of enterprise platforms at mid-market pricing, with seasonal talent pool management no other competitor owns.

---

## Key Pain Point (#1)

**The problem:**
Group-level HR cannot see which locations have staffing crises until a manager calls them in panic. No pipeline. No early warning. Decisions are reactive. When summer peak arrives and three locations are simultaneously understaffed, the problem is already a revenue crisis.

**Customer language:**
*"I can't see which location has a bottleneck until someone rings me."* — Siobhán, Group People Director, 18-location restaurant/hotel group

**Why it's unsolved:**
- Enterprise platforms (Harri, Fourth) solve this but at pricing ($875+/month) that excludes mid-market operators
- SMB tools (Workstream, StaffedUp) are US-only single-site tools with no multi-location support
- Hireology has multi-location visibility but no transparent pricing and is US-centric
- The informal stack (spreadsheets, email, WhatsApp) has no pipeline visibility at all

---

## Product Promise (One Sentence)

Lapam-ATS gives multi-location hospitality groups pipeline visibility, mobile-first candidate engagement, and seasonal talent pool management — at pricing that doesn't require board approval.

---

## Core Differentiators (3–4 Reasons This Product Wins vs Alternatives)

### 1. Seasonal Talent Pool CRM
**What it does:** Re-engage last season's good workers with batch outreach before starting cold sourcing. Tag departing seasonal employees as "re-hire eligible" at contract close. Send a batch re-engagement message to 200+ past workers with an availability form. Arrive at March with a partially filled roster instead of an empty one.

**Why it wins:** No other ATS in the hospitality segment owns this feature. Eploy (UK-only) has partial seasonal CRM functionality. Harri, Hireology, Workstream, StaffedUp — none have a talent pool designed for seasonal re-engagement.

**Proof point:** Claire persona (6 coastal properties, sole HR): *"I spent three weeks last March ringing through a spreadsheet of 200 people I hired two years ago to find out who was available. Two of them had moved. Forty didn't answer. Twenty said yes. That's my talent pool."* Operators pay 15–25% agency markup for peak season coverage; a CRM that substitutes this is ROI-positive in the first season.

---

### 2. Multi-Location Pipeline Dashboard at Accessible Pricing
**What it does:** One-screen view of hiring status across every location in the group. Open roles, applications per stage, days since last activity, visual alerts on stalled locations. Real-time updates. Accessible to Group Admin (sees all locations) and Location Managers (scoped to their sites only).

**Why it wins:** Harri and Fourth have this feature but at enterprise pricing ($875+/month for Harri; Fourth custom-priced). Hireology has multi-location visibility but no transparent pricing. Workstream, StaffedUp, HigherMe are US-only single-site tools. The gap: mid-market international operators (5–50 locations, £300–600/month budget) have no credible multi-location ATS option.

**Proof point:** Yusuf persona (8-location owner-operator, no dedicated HR) looked at Harri: *"The demo was great but they wanted more per month than we make on a slow Tuesday."* Lapam-ATS delivers the enterprise feature at mid-market pricing.

---

### 3. UK/EU Compliance Architecture from Day One
**What it does:** GDPR consent captured at application as a first-class data entity. Automated retention and deletion policies. UK right-to-work verification workflow with GBG IDVT integration and immutable audit records. Multi-jurisdiction compliance (UK, EU, US roadmap) built into the data model — not bolted on.

**Why it wins:** Every US-native competitor (Workstream, Fountain, Hireology, StaffedUp, HigherMe, CareerPlug) lacks meaningful EU/UK compliance investment. They are geography-limited by regulatory gap, not by choice. A product with native GDPR architecture, UK right-to-work workflow, and EU AI Act-compliant screening cannot be quickly replicated because the compliance investment is not just engineering — it is legal expertise, regulatory relationships, and ongoing monitoring. This is a structural moat.

**Proof point:** GDPR fines up to 4% of global turnover. UK right-to-work fines £45,000–£60,000 per illegal worker. Siobhán persona: *"The GDPR thing keeps me up at night — if someone submits an SAR about a rejected application, I genuinely don't know if we can demonstrate compliance."*

---

### 4. Mobile-First No-Login Candidate Application
**What it does:** Candidates apply via a unique job link or QR code in under 3 minutes. No account creation. No login wall. Application completes in 4 or fewer screens on mobile. GDPR consent captured inline. Automated acknowledgement sent within 2 minutes via SMS or email.

**Why it wins:** Mobile-first with no login is not a feature — it is the price of entry for candidate engagement in the EU/UK market. Harri and Fourth both require account creation or multi-step processes. Workstream has no-login mobile apply but is US-only. Lapam-ATS delivers this for the UK/EU candidate pool.

**Proof point:** 80% of hospitality job seekers conduct entire search on mobile (Hireology 2024). ~70% of traffic lost when login/account creation required (Hireology CEO, Nov 2024). 73% abandon applications too long or complex on mobile. Aisha persona (22-year-old candidate, London) applied to six places; only one responded within the hour via WhatsApp — she took that job.

## Why Now? (Market Timing)

1. **EU AI Act enforcement begins February 2025** — any AI screening feature in the EU requires human-in-the-loop and audit infrastructure. US-native competitors lack this; Lapam-ATS is EU AI Act-ready from day one.

2. **UK right-to-work fines increased** — £45,000–£60,000 per illegal worker. Compliance risk is no longer discretionary; operators need audit-ready systems.

3. **Post-pandemic turnover structurally elevated** — 70–80% annual turnover in US hospitality (BLS); similar patterns in UK/EU. High churn makes cost-per-hire reduction tools ROI-positive immediately.

4. **Seasonal hiring window compression** — operators recruit 2–3 months ahead of peak; any delay costs revenue. Tools that accelerate sourcing and re-engagement have direct P&L impact.

5. **Informal stacks failing under scale** — multi-location groups have hit the ceiling of spreadsheets and WhatsApp. Enterprise tools are too expensive. The mid-market is underserved and ready for a purpose-built solution.
