# Solution One-Pager: Owner-Operator (No Dedicated HR)

**Persona:** Yusuf — Operations Director / Co-owner, 8-location casual dining group, Midlands and North England  
**Context:** Co-owner; no dedicated HR; site managers handle their own hiring; needs oversight without becoming a bottleneck  
**Primary use case:** One-screen visibility across all 8 sites without making 8 phone calls every Monday morning

---

## Your #1 Challenge

You and your business partner opened your first site in 2009. You now run 8 locations across the Midlands and North England. You've never had dedicated HR. Site managers handle their own hiring with Indeed and social media.

You approve CVs for supervisor and assistant manager roles. Everything else, they handle. The problem is: you have no idea how much hiring is costing you across the group, how long positions typically go unfilled, or which sites have chronic staffing problems vs occasional openings.

You just know that two of your sites have been short-staffed for weeks, and it's showing up in Google reviews. Customers are complaining about slow service. Your managers are working double shifts to cover the gaps. But you don't know about these problems until a manager calls you — usually on a Saturday when it's too late to fix it.

You've been saying for two years that you'll "fix the hiring process." The reality is: no one has time to set something up. And every system you've looked at wants you to spend a week on implementation, attend training webinars, and integrate with five other tools you don't use.

You need something that shows you the problem **before it becomes a crisis**, without requiring you to become a full-time HR manager.

### Delegate Hiring to Your Managers Without Losing Oversight

Your site managers are good operators. They're not good HR people. You need a tool that makes it **easy for them** to hire independently, while giving you **oversight** without micromanagement.

Lapam-ATS does this with **role-based access control**:
- **Site managers** see only their location's pipeline. They can post jobs, review applications, schedule interviews, and send offers — all from their phone.
- **You (Owner/Group Admin)** see all 8 locations on one dashboard. You can drill down into any site's pipeline, see individual candidates, and step in when needed — but you're not required to be in the loop for every hire.

Your managers are empowered to hire independently. You're not a bottleneck. But you have **visibility** when you need it.

### Know What Hiring Costs You

Your business partner asked you last quarter: *"How much does it cost us to hire across the group?"* You didn't have an answer. You still don't.

With Lapam-ATS, you open the **analytics view** and see:
- **Time-to-fill by location:** Birmingham: 9 days average; Manchester: 18 days average; Leeds: 6 days average
- **Application volume by source:** Indeed: 65%; direct link: 20%; referral: 15%
- **Pipeline conversion rates:** Applied → Interviewed: 35%; Interviewed → Offered: 60%; Offered → Hired: 80%
- **Cost-per-hire estimate:** Monthly subscription cost ÷ 8 locations ÷ hires in period = approximate platform cost per hire (external costs like job board spend excluded but tracked separately)

You now know:
- Manchester takes **3x longer** to fill roles than Leeds (18 days vs 6 days)
- 15% of your hires come from **employee referrals** (should you incentivise this more?)
- Your **pipeline conversion rate** from interview to offer is strong (60%) — your managers are good at assessing candidates

This is the data your accountant and your business partner want to see. You're no longer guessing. You're managing hiring as a **measurable business function**.

### Self-Service Onboarding: No Training Webinars, No Implementation Project

Every system you've looked at requires:
- A 90-minute demo with a sales rep
- A 2-week "implementation project"
- Integration with your scheduling tool, your payroll tool, your email system
- Training webinars for your managers

You don't have time for this. Neither do your managers. If the tool requires more than 30 minutes to get started, it won't get used.

Lapam-ATS is **self-service from day one**:
1. Sign up with your email (Clerk authentication — no password complexity requirements)
2. Add your 8 locations (name, country, optional timezone)
3. Invite your site managers (they receive an email invite; accept and start using immediately)
4. Create your first job posting from a role template (server, chef, bartender, kitchen porter)
5. Publish to Indeed

Your first job is live **within 30 minutes** of signing up. No demo required. No training webinar. If your managers can use WhatsApp, they can use Lapam-ATS.

### No Integration Dependency. Clean Handoff to Your Existing Tools.

You use Deputy for scheduling. You use Sage for payroll. You use Xero for accounting. You don't want to rip out your existing tools and replace them with a bundled platform.

Lapam-ATS is **ATS-only**. It does not do scheduling, time and attendance, or payroll. When a candidate is hired, the system hands off cleanly to Deputy (WFM integration) and Sage/Xero (payroll data export). Your existing tools stay in place. Lapam-ATS fills the hiring gap — nothing more.

This is the opposite of Harri and Fourth, which require you to adopt their entire workforce platform. You keep what works. You replace only what's broken.

## Proof Point: Unfilled Roles Are Revenue Leakage

You run 8 casual dining locations. Each location has approximately 40 staff. Assume 70% annual turnover (hospitality sector average):

- 8 locations × 40 staff = 320 total staff
- 320 × 70% turnover = **224 hires per year**
- Average time-to-fill (current informal process): 3 weeks per role
- 224 hires × 3 weeks = **672 weeks of unfilled positions per year** (13 years of unfilled capacity)

Every unfilled position is revenue leakage. A server position unfilled for 3 weeks means:
- Reduced table capacity (fewer tables served)
- Overtime for remaining staff (time-and-a-half)
- Service quality issues (slower service, lower tips, worse reviews)

Assume each unfilled role costs the business **£300 per week** in lost revenue and overtime:

- 672 weeks of unfilled positions × £300 = **£201,600 per year**

If Lapam-ATS reduces your average time-to-fill from **3 weeks to 2 weeks** (faster candidate engagement, no applications lost in email, proactive alerts on stalled pipelines), you save:

- 224 hires × 1 week saved = **224 weeks of unfilled capacity eliminated**
- 224 weeks × £300 = **£67,200 in annual savings**

Lapam-ATS costs you **£4,800 per year** (8 locations × £50/location/month × 12 months). The platform pays for itself **14 times over** in year one.
