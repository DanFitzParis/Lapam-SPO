Battle Card: Hireology

Competitor: Hireology (hireology.com) — Multi-location ATS for select verticals (hospitality, automotive, healthcare, fitness)

Target segment: Multi-location operators, franchise groups

Geography: US-primary

Pricing: Custom (Essentials and Professional tiers); no public pricing

Overview

Hireology was named #1 hotel ATS in the 2024 HotelTechAwards and placed in the top 10 across 200+ G2 Winter 2024 reports. It focuses on the hiring funnel — sourcing to onboarding — without deep WFM integration. Strong multi-location management and candidate communication automation. US-centric product with limited international compliance support.

When Hireology Wins

  • US-based operators: Single-country US operators who don't need EU/UK compliance
  • Feature breadth over compliance depth: Operators who prioritise skills testing, 50+ job board integrations, and advanced screening over compliance infrastructure
  • Franchise groups: Multi-brand franchise operations where Hireology's multi-vertical support (hospitality, automotive, healthcare) is an advantage
  • Established product confidence: Risk-averse buyers who want a proven, mature product (#1 hotel ATS 2024) over a zero-to-one launch

When Lapam-ATS Wins

  • UK/EU operators: Any operator hiring across the UK, Ireland, France, Germany, Spain, or multi-jurisdiction EU. Hireology does not have GDPR or UK right-to-work compliance.
  • Pricing transparency requirement: Operators who will not engage with a sales team for custom pricing; need to know cost before the first call
  • Seasonal operations: Coastal hotels, ski resorts, event venues — any operator who re-hires the same population year after year. Hireology has no seasonal CRM.
  • Hospitality-only focus: Operators who want a tool purpose-built for hospitality, not a multi-vertical product (Hireology also serves automotive and healthcare)
Key positioning message: "Built for the UK and EU hospitality market — not a US tool with EU aspirations."

Proof Points

Geographic Limitation

Hireology is US-primary with no documented EU compliance support. International compliance architecture (GDPR consent lifecycle, UK right-to-work, EU AI Act) is not featured in product documentation. Operators in the UK/EU cannot adopt a US-native tool without accepting material compliance risk.

Source: Competitors.md; Hireology product documentation

Pricing Opacity

Hireology uses custom pricing with no public rates. Buyers must engage with sales to receive a quote. Lapam-ATS publishes transparent location-based pricing (£50–60/location/month). Yusuf persona (8-location owner-operator): "I looked at [Harri] once. The demo was great but they wanted more per month than we make on a slow Tuesday." Pricing transparency is a buying trigger for mid-market operators.

Source: Competitors.md; ICP-and-personas.md

Seasonal CRM Gap

Hireology has candidate pool management but not seasonal talent pool re-engagement. Claire persona (6 coastal properties): "I spent three weeks last March manually ringing through a spreadsheet of 200 people I hired two years ago." Lapam-ATS is the only hospitality ATS with batch re-engagement and talent pool consent lifecycle.

Source: ICP-and-personas.md; competitive analysis

Feature Maturity Trade-Off

Hireology is a mature product with broad feature set. Lapam-ATS is a zero-to-one product with MVP feature set. This is a genuine trade-off. Operators who need skills testing, 50+ job boards, and advanced screening should choose Hireology (if US-based). Operators who need UK/EU compliance depth and seasonal CRM should choose Lapam-ATS.

Source: Product positioning framework

Objection Handling

Objection: "Hireology is the #1 hotel ATS. Why would we choose a zero-to-one product?"

Response: "That's a fair question — and Hireology deserves that recognition. It's a mature, feature-complete product. But it's built for the US market. If you're a US-based operator, Hireology is probably the right choice. But if you're operating in the UK, Ireland, or the EU, Hireology doesn't have the compliance architecture you legally require: GDPR consent capture at application, UK right-to-work verification with GBG IDVT, EU AI Act-ready screening. We're not trying to out-feature Hireology. We're solving the compliance gap they haven't addressed."

Objection: "Hireology has skills testing. You don't."

Response: "You're right — skills testing is not in our MVP scope. We prioritised the features that the research told us are purchase-level pain for UK/EU operators: multi-location visibility, seasonal CRM, and compliance depth. If skills testing is critical to your hiring process, Hireology is a better fit (if you're US-based). But if your hiring decisions are made in interviews and trial shifts — which is the pattern we saw across every persona we interviewed — skills testing is a nice-to-have, not a launch blocker."

Objection: "What if we expand to the US? Will you support us?"

Response: "US market launch is on our roadmap for 12 months post-launch (Q2 2027). We'll add I-9/E-Verify compliance and EEOC reporting when we enter the US. If you're planning US expansion in the next 6 months and you need a tool that works in both markets today, Hireology may be the safer choice. But if your core operations are UK/EU and US expansion is 12+ months out, we'll be ready when you are."
