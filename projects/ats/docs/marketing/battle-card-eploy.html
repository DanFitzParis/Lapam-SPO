Battle Card: Eploy

Competitor: Eploy (eploy.com) — UK hospitality ATS + Talent CRM

Target segment: Mid-to-large UK hospitality groups, 200–5,000 staff

Geography: UK-primary

Pricing: Custom; described as competitive on "price and scalability" (no public pricing)

Overview

Eploy is a UK-born ATS with a dedicated hospitality sector product. Active client base in UK hospitality, healthcare, and leisure. Differentiator: Talent CRM combined with ATS — allows operators to build talent pools for seasonal re-engagement rather than starting sourcing from zero each peak season. Microsoft integration is a stated feature. Scalable: designed for organisations from mid-market upward. Eploy is the closest competitor to Lapam-ATS in terms of feature set and hospitality-native focus — but it is UK-only and lacks the mobile-first candidate engagement of newer platforms.

When Eploy Wins

  • Proven UK hospitality track record: Established client base in UK hospitality, healthcare, and leisure; less risk for risk-averse buyers
  • Large estate deployment: Operators managing 1,000–5,000 staff across multiple locations who need enterprise-grade scalability (3,500-staff deployment documented)
  • Microsoft ecosystem requirement: Operators who need native Microsoft integration (calendar, communication) and won't switch to Google Workspace or other tools
  • Multi-sector hiring: Operators hiring across hospitality, healthcare, and leisure (Eploy serves all three); Lapam-ATS is hospitality-only

When Lapam-ATS Wins

  • International operations: Operators hiring across UK + EU (France, Germany, Spain, Ireland). Eploy is UK-primary; Lapam-ATS is multi-jurisdiction from day one.
  • Mobile-first candidate engagement requirement: Operators who lose candidates because the application process is too slow or too desktop-centric. Eploy's candidate-facing UX is not a documented strength.
  • Pricing transparency requirement: Operators who will not engage with a sales team for custom pricing; need to know cost before the first call. Eploy pricing is opaque.
  • Seasonal CRM as primary buying trigger: Operators who want the most purpose-built seasonal re-engagement workflow. Eploy has talent CRM but it's not as deeply optimised for seasonal hospitality as Lapam-ATS.
  • Self-service onboarding preference: Operators who need to be live within 30 minutes of signing up, not 2–4 weeks of implementation. Eploy has a learning curve.
Key positioning message: "The seasonal CRM depth of Eploy — with mobile-first candidate engagement, transparent pricing, and multi-jurisdiction compliance."

Proof Points

Seasonal CRM Parity

Eploy is the only other hospitality ATS with seasonal talent pool CRM. Research: "Talent CRM combined with ATS — allows operators to build talent pools for seasonal re-engagement." This is Eploy's strongest differentiator. Lapam-ATS matches this feature with batch re-engagement, talent pool consent lifecycle, and re-hire eligibility tagging. The competitive battle is not whether seasonal CRM exists — it's which implementation is more purpose-built.

Source: Competitors.md; Eploy product documentation

Mobile-First Gap

Research: "Limited mobile/candidate-facing innovation" — Eploy's candidate UX is not a documented strength. Aisha persona (22-year-old candidate, London, applies on iPhone): "Why do I need to create an account just to apply for a weekend shift?" 70% of traffic is lost when login/account creation is required (Hireology CEO, Nov 2024). Lapam-ATS delivers no-login mobile application completing in under 3 minutes.

Source: Competitors.md; ICP-and-personas.md

UK-Only Limitation

Eploy is UK-primary with limited evidence of global deployment. Lapam-ATS is UK-first but multi-jurisdiction from day one (GDPR consent lifecycle, UK right-to-work, EU AI Act-ready). For operators hiring across UK + France, UK + Ireland, or UK + Spain, Lapam-ATS is the only option that delivers both seasonal CRM and multi-jurisdiction compliance.

Source: Competitors.md; Lapam-ATS positioning framework

Pricing Transparency

Eploy uses custom pricing with no public rates. One reviewer chose Eploy over alternatives on "price and scalability" but did not disclose the actual price. Lapam-ATS publishes transparent location-based pricing (£50–60/location/month). For a 10-location group, that's £500–£600/month — within discretionary budget for mid-market operators.

Objection Handling

Objection: "Eploy is proven in UK hospitality. You're zero-to-one. Why take the risk?"

Response: "That's a fair question — and if you're a UK-only operator with no plans to expand internationally, Eploy is a proven, low-risk choice. It's been in the UK market for years and has a strong hospitality client base. But if you're hiring across the UK and the EU — or you plan to — Eploy is UK-primary and doesn't have the multi-jurisdiction compliance architecture you need. We're not trying to replace Eploy in the UK-only segment. We're solving the international compliance and mobile-first candidate engagement gaps they haven't addressed."

Objection: "Eploy already has seasonal CRM. What makes yours better?"

Response: "Eploy's talent CRM is a strong feature — and it's one of the reasons we respect them as a competitor. The difference is purpose. Eploy's talent CRM is a general-purpose feature that works across hospitality, healthcare, and leisure. Lapam-ATS's seasonal CRM is built specifically for the hospitality seasonal cycle: tag departing workers as 're-hire eligible' at contract close, send batch re-engagement messages in early February, track availability by property and role, manage talent pool consent separately from application consent. We've optimised for the exact workflow Claire (our seasonal HR persona) runs every spring. If Eploy's CRM meets your needs, great. If you want something more purpose-built for seasonal hospitality, we'd love to show you the difference."

Objection: "We need Microsoft integration. You don't have it."

Response: "You're right — Microsoft integration is not a launch feature for us. It's on our roadmap (Outlook calendar sync for interview scheduling), but it's not shipping at MVP. If native Microsoft integration is critical to your workflow and you won't consider Google Workspace or other tools, Eploy is a better fit. But if you can work with SMS/email interview scheduling and Twilio-based candidate communication for now, and you need mobile-first candidate engagement and multi-jurisdiction compliance, we're the stronger choice. Most of our target operators don't use Microsoft 365 heavily — they're managing hiring from their phones, not from Outlook."
