# Press Release: Lapam-ATS Launches Multi-Location Hiring Platform to Help UK Hospitality Groups Stop Losing Candidates in the Gaps

## Seasonal talent pool management, multi-location pipeline visibility, and UK compliance architecture deliver the visibility of enterprise platforms at mid-market pricing

---

**LONDON, UK — 15 June 2026** — Lapam today launched Lapam-ATS, a multi-location hiring platform built specifically for independent hospitality groups operating 5–50 locations across multiple countries. The platform delivers multi-location pipeline visibility, seasonal talent pool management, and UK/EU compliance infrastructure at pricing accessible to mid-market operators — addressing a segment currently underserved by enterprise platforms priced for 500+ location chains.

Hospitality groups hiring 200–2,000 people per year across 10, 20, or 50 locations face a structural problem: the informal stack — spreadsheets, email, WhatsApp groups, and job board subscriptions — cannot scale. Group-level HR cannot see which locations have staffing crises until a manager calls them. Candidates who apply on mobile take another job within hours if they receive no response. GDPR consent is not captured. UK right-to-work checks are done on paper with no audit trail. The informal stack cannot survive a compliance audit, and enterprise platforms like Harri and Fourth are priced out of reach at $875+/month or custom enterprise pricing.

Lapam-ATS solves three specific, costly failures: visibility failure (no pipeline across locations), speed failure (candidates lost to faster-responding competitors), and compliance failure (audit risk on GDPR and UK right-to-work).

---

## The Problem: Three Pain Points Hospitality Groups Face Every Day

### 1. No Visibility Across Locations
Group People Directors and owner-operators managing 10+ locations cannot see which sites have open roles, how many applications are in progress, or which pipelines are stalled — until a manager calls them. There is no dashboard. No early warning. Decisions are reactive. When summer peak arrives and three locations are simultaneously understaffed, the problem is already a revenue crisis.

Siobhán Kelly, Group People Director at a 18-location UK/Ireland restaurant and hotel group, describes the problem: *"I can't see which location has a bottleneck until someone rings me. By the time I know there's a problem, it's too late to fix it before the weekend service."*

### 2. Candidates Take Another Job Within Hours
The hospitality candidate applies to 4–6 jobs on mobile within an hour. If an operator takes 2–3 days to respond because applications are stuck in email, the candidate has already accepted another offer. Research shows 80% of hospitality job seekers conduct their entire search on mobile, and 70% abandon applications that require account creation or login. The window to engage is measured in hours, not days. The informal stack loses candidates in the gaps between tools.

### 3. Compliance Audit Risk on GDPR and UK Right to Work
The informal stack — CVs in email, WhatsApp groups, paper right-to-work documents — cannot demonstrate GDPR consent capture, data retention policies, or audit-ready right-to-work verification. A single compliance audit or data subject access request surfaces this immediately. GDPR fines reach up to 4% of global turnover. UK right-to-work fines are £45,000–£60,000 per illegal worker. Compliance is not discretionary risk — it is legal liability.

Claire Thompson, Head of HR at a 6-property UK coastal hotel group, explains: *"The GDPR thing keeps me up at night. If someone submits a data subject access request about a rejected application, I genuinely don't know if we can demonstrate compliance. We're holding CVs from four years ago and I haven't gotten proper consent for most of them."*

## The Solution: Multi-Location Visibility, Seasonal CRM, and Compliance Depth at Mid-Market Pricing

Lapam-ATS is a standalone, multi-tenant SaaS platform that delivers three core capabilities:

### 1. Multi-Location Pipeline Dashboard
Group Admins see the hiring status for all locations on a single screen: open roles per location, application counts per stage, days since last activity, and visual alerts on stalled pipelines. Real-time updates. Location Managers see only their assigned sites. No more Monday morning phone calls to eight different GMs. No more discovering a staffing crisis when a manager rings at 9pm on Saturday.

Yusuf Ahmed, Operations Director and co-owner of an 8-location casual dining group in the Midlands and North England, describes the impact: *"I used to make eight phone calls every Monday morning to find out which sites had hiring problems. Now I log in on Sunday evening and see the whole picture on one screen. I know which locations need help before the week starts."*

### 2. Seasonal Talent Pool CRM
Re-engage last season's best workers with batch outreach before starting cold sourcing. When a seasonal employee departs at contract close, managers tag them as "re-hire eligible", "conditional re-hire", or "do not re-engage". The system adds them to the organisation's talent pool with separate talent pool consent. In early February, operators send a batch re-engagement message to 200+ past workers with an availability form. They arrive at March with a partially filled roster instead of an empty one.

Claire Thompson, whose 6 coastal properties close November–March and reopen with 450 staff by mid-June, describes the seasonal hiring problem: *"I spent three weeks last March manually ringing through a spreadsheet of 200 people I hired two years ago to find out who was available. Two of them had moved. Forty didn't answer. Twenty said yes. That's my talent pool."*

With Lapam-ATS, she now sends one batch message and tracks responses in the platform. *"I re-hired 60% of my summer roster before I even touched Indeed. That's three weeks of my life back, and the people I hired were already trained."*

Operators currently pay 15–25% agency markup for peak season coverage. A talent pool CRM that substitutes agency spend is ROI-positive in the first season.

### 3. UK Right-to-Work Verification and GDPR Compliance Infrastructure
When a candidate is moved to "Offer" stage for a UK role, the system initiates the right-to-work verification workflow. The candidate receives a mobile link (no login required) and submits their share code or document via GBG IDVT integration. The system returns a pass or fail result and creates an immutable, timestamped audit record. If the check covers a time-limited permission (visa or BRP), the system schedules an alert 30 days before expiry.

GDPR consent is captured at point of application as a first-class data entity. The system automatically deletes candidate records 12 months after last activity (unsuccessful candidates) or triggers re-consent workflows when consent expires. When a Group Admin receives a data subject access request, the system generates a machine-readable JSON export within 24 hours. Right-to-erasure requests are processed within 72 hours.

Siobhán Kelly describes the compliance confidence this delivers: *"When our legal team asked me to demonstrate our GDPR compliance posture, I clicked one button and handed them an audit report. Before Lapam-ATS, I would have spent a week pulling data from email inboxes and spreadsheets."*

## Customer Experience: From Job Posting to First Shift in 48 Hours

Marco Rossi is the General Manager of a 3-location Italian restaurant group in the Netherlands (Amsterdam, Rotterdam, Utrecht). He has no dedicated HR. He is the manager, the scheduler, and the recruiter. He needs to hire two servers for the Amsterdam location before the weekend.

**Monday, 2pm:** Marco opens Lapam-ATS on his phone between lunch and dinner service. He selects a "Server" role template. The system generates a job description draft using AI in 10 seconds. Marco edits the hours and pay rate, adds two screening questions ("Do you have experience with Italian wine service?" and "Can you work Friday and Saturday evenings?"), and publishes the job. The system posts to Indeed and generates a QR code. Marco prints the QR code and tapes it to the restaurant's front window.

**Monday, 3pm:** A candidate walking past the restaurant scans the QR code on her phone. The application loads instantly. No login. No account creation. She completes the application in 3 minutes: name, mobile number, email, availability (part-time), answers to the two screening questions, GDPR consent checkbox. She submits. Within 2 minutes, she receives an automated SMS acknowledgement: *"Thanks for applying to Amsterdam Server at Marco's Trattoria. We'll review your application and be in touch within 24 hours."*

**Monday, 5pm:** Marco checks his phone between services. He sees the application on the Lapam-ATS dashboard. The candidate answered both screening questions favourably (yes to wine service experience, yes to Friday/Saturday availability). Marco taps "Send Interview Invitation" and proposes three interview slots: Tuesday 3pm, Wednesday 11am, Wednesday 3pm. The candidate receives the options via SMS. She selects Wednesday 11am. Both Marco and the candidate receive confirmation messages. The system sends a reminder to both 24 hours before the interview.

**Wednesday, 11am:** Marco interviews the candidate. She's a strong fit. He moves her to "Offer" stage in the app and sends a digital offer letter (€13.50/hour, part-time contract, Friday and Saturday evenings, start date Friday). The candidate receives the offer on her phone. She reviews and accepts with a tap. Marco receives a confirmation notification.

**Wednesday, 2pm:** Because the role is in the Netherlands (not UK), no right-to-work verification workflow is triggered (Netherlands is not in launch scope for RTW). Marco sends the onboarding documents via the app: contract for signature, bank details form, emergency contact. The candidate completes them on mobile. Marco marks her as "Hired" in the system.

**Friday, 5pm:** The new hire arrives for her first shift. She's already in Marco's Deputy scheduling system (synced via the WFM handoff integration). She's trained by the senior server and works the Friday evening service. Marco hired and onboarded a qualified candidate in 72 hours, entirely from his phone, with no desktop steps.

## "I Re-Hired 60% of My Summer Roster Before Touching Indeed"

Claire Thompson's 6 coastal hotel properties close every November and reopen in late March. Her peak-season workforce is 450 people. Her off-season skeleton crew is 80. Every spring, she rebuilds the workforce from scratch. She used to spend three weeks in March manually ringing through a spreadsheet of 200 past workers to find out who was available for the summer.

With Lapam-ATS, she now tags departing workers at contract close in October. "Re-hire eligible" workers (the ones who performed well and want to return) are added to the talent pool with separate consent. In early February, Claire sends a batch re-engagement message via the platform: *"We're opening again in late March. Interested in coming back? Confirm your availability here."* The message includes a link to a short availability form (which dates, which property, which role).

Within two weeks, 120 of the 200 past workers respond. 80 confirm full availability. 40 confirm partial availability (specific weeks only). Claire hires 60% of her summer roster before ever posting a job to Indeed. The remaining 40% she sources through new recruitment — but she's starting from a partially filled roster, not an empty one.

*"That's three weeks of my life back,"* Claire said. *"And the people I re-hired were already trained. They knew the properties. They knew the systems. First-week productivity was 10x higher than new hires. The seasonal CRM alone justified the cost of Lapam-ATS."*
