# Marketing Brief: Lapam-ATS

**Purpose:** Internal context document for all marketing materials. Synthesises approved research and specification documents into positioning, messaging, and differentiation.

---

## Positioning Statement

For independent hospitality groups operating 5–50 locations across multiple countries, Lapam-ATS is a multi-location hiring platform that delivers pipeline visibility, mobile-first candidate engagement, and seasonal talent pool management unlike spreadsheets and job boards which cannot scale across locations or survive a compliance audit.

---

## Top 3 Pain Points (Ranked)

### 1. Visibility Failure Across Locations
Group HR directors and owner-operators cannot see which locations have staffing crises until a manager calls them in panic. No pipeline. No early warning. Decisions are reactive. When summer peak arrives and three locations are simultaneously understaffed, the problem is already a revenue crisis.

**Evidence:** Siobhán persona (18-location group People Director): *"I can't see which location has a bottleneck until someone rings me"*. Yusuf persona (8-location owner-operator): *"I don't know what it costs us to hire. I genuinely don't. That's embarrassing."*

### 2. Speed-to-Contact Window Measured in Hours
Hospitality candidates apply to multiple jobs simultaneously on mobile. If an operator takes 2–3 days to respond, the candidate has already accepted another offer. The informal stack (email routing, spreadsheet tracking) loses candidates in the gaps between tools. Every lost candidate extends the time a position remains unfilled — and every unfilled position is lost revenue.

**Evidence:** 80% of hospitality job seekers conduct their entire search on mobile (Hireology 2024). Candidates abandon applications that require account creation (~70% traffic loss) or take longer than 3 minutes. Industry pattern: *"If you don't respond within the first few hours, they've already taken another job"* (Workstream, StaffedUp operational data).

### 3. Compliance Audit Risk (GDPR, UK Right to Work)
The informal stack — CVs in email, WhatsApp groups, paper right-to-work documents — cannot demonstrate GDPR consent capture, data retention policies, or audit-ready right-to-work verification. A single compliance audit or data subject access request surfaces this immediately. Fines: £45,000–£60,000 per illegal worker (UK), up to 4% of global turnover (GDPR).

**Evidence:** Siobhán persona: *"The GDPR thing keeps me up at night — if someone submits an SAR about a rejected application, I genuinely don't know if we can demonstrate compliance"*. UK right-to-work is mandatory before first shift; failure is not discretionary risk — it is legal liability.

## Key Differentiators (What Lapam-ATS Does That Competitors Don't)

### 1. Seasonal Talent Pool CRM
Re-engage last season's good workers with batch outreach before starting cold sourcing. Tag departing seasonal employees as "re-hire eligible" at contract close. In February, send a batch re-engagement message to 200+ past workers with an availability form. Arrive at March with a partially filled roster instead of an empty one.

**Why this wins:** No other ATS in the hospitality segment owns this feature. Eploy (UK-only) has partial seasonal CRM functionality. Harri, Hireology, Workstream, StaffedUp — none have a talent pool designed for seasonal re-engagement. Claire persona (6 coastal properties, sole HR): *"I spent three weeks last March ringing through a spreadsheet of 200 people I hired two years ago to find out who was available. Two of them had moved. Forty didn't answer. Twenty said yes. That's my talent pool."* A CRM that turns this manual chaos into a single batch action is a purchase-level trigger for seasonal operators.

### 2. Multi-Location Pipeline Dashboard at Accessible Pricing
One-screen view of hiring status across every location in the group. Open roles, applications per stage, days since last activity, visual alerts on stalled locations. Real-time updates. Accessible to Group Admin (sees all locations) and Location Managers (scoped to their sites only).

**Why this wins:** Harri and Fourth have this feature but at enterprise pricing ($875+/month for Harri; Fourth custom-priced). Hireology has multi-location visibility but no transparent pricing. Workstream, StaffedUp, HigherMe are US-only single-site tools. The gap: mid-market international operators (5–50 locations, £300–600/month budget) have no credible multi-location ATS option. Lapam-ATS delivers the enterprise feature at mid-market pricing.

### 3. UK/EU Compliance Architecture from Day One
GDPR consent captured at application as a first-class data entity. Automated retention and deletion policies. UK right-to-work verification workflow with GBG IDVT integration and immutable audit records. Multi-jurisdiction compliance (UK, EU, US roadmap) built into the data model — not bolted on.

**Why this wins:** Every US-native competitor (Workstream, Fountain, Hireology, StaffedUp, HigherMe, CareerPlug) lacks meaningful EU/UK compliance investment. They are geography-limited by regulatory gap, not by choice. A product with native GDPR architecture, UK right-to-work workflow, and EU AI Act-compliant screening cannot be quickly replicated because the compliance investment is not just engineering — it is legal expertise, regulatory relationships, and ongoing monitoring. This is a structural moat.

### 4. Mobile-First No-Login Candidate Application
Candidates apply via a unique job link or QR code in under 3 minutes. No account creation. No login wall. Application completes in 4 or fewer screens on mobile. GDPR consent captured inline. Automated acknowledgement sent within 2 minutes via SMS or email.

**Why this wins:** Aisha persona (22-year-old candidate, London, applies on iPhone): *"Why do I need to create an account just to apply for a weekend shift?"* Mobile-first with no login is not a feature — it is the price of entry for candidate engagement in the EU/UK market. Harri and Fourth both require account creation or multi-step processes. Workstream has no-login mobile apply but is US-only. Lapam-ATS delivers this for the UK/EU candidate pool.

## Competitive Positioning

### vs Harri (Enterprise WFM + ATS)
**Harri wins on:** Feature completeness, candidate sharing across locations, forecast-based scheduling integration  
**Lapam-ATS wins on:** Price (Harri starts at $875/month for 100 employees; Lapam-ATS targets £300–600/month for 5–50 locations), seasonal CRM (Harri does not have this), global payroll (Harri payroll is UK-only; Lapam-ATS is ATS-only with clean handoff to any payroll system)  
**Positioning:** *"The multi-location visibility and seasonal hiring power of Harri — at a price that doesn't require board approval."*

### vs Hireology (Multi-location ATS, US-centric)
**Hireology wins on:** Feature maturity (#1 hotel ATS, HotelTechAwards 2024), SMS communication, skills testing  
**Lapam-ATS wins on:** EU/UK compliance (Hireology is US-primary with no documented EU compliance), transparent pricing (Hireology custom-priced), seasonal CRM (Hireology does not have this)  
**Positioning:** *"Built for the UK and EU hospitality market — not a US tool with EU aspirations."*

### vs Fourth/HotSchedules (Enterprise WFM + ATS)
**Fourth wins on:** WFM integration depth, labour forecasting, PEO compliance wrapper, enterprise scale  
**Lapam-ATS wins on:** Price (Fourth is enterprise/custom-priced), standalone ATS (Fourth is bundled platform), seasonal CRM  
**Positioning:** *"An ATS that solves your hiring problem — not a £100k+ workforce platform you don't need yet."*

### vs Workstream/StaffedUp/HigherMe (US SMB tools)
**They win on:** Simplicity, transparent pricing, mobile-first UX  
**Lapam-ATS wins on:** Geography (they are US-only; Lapam-ATS is UK/EU-native), multi-location support (they are single-site tools), compliance (GDPR + UK RTW), seasonal CRM  
**Positioning:** *"The simplicity of Workstream — built for operators hiring across the UK and Europe, not just the US."*

### vs Informal Stack (Spreadsheets + WhatsApp + Indeed)
**The informal stack wins on:** Zero monthly cost, zero onboarding, managers already know how to use it  
**Lapam-ATS wins on:** Multi-location visibility (spreadsheets have none), speed (automated acknowledgement vs 2–3 day email lag), compliance (the informal stack cannot survive a GDPR audit), seasonal CRM (manually ringing 200 people vs one batch action)  
**Positioning:** *"What you're doing now — but with a dashboard that shows you problems before they become crises."*

## Market Context (For Internal Use)

- **Global hospitality ATS market:** $2.9B (2024), growing to $6.31B by 2033
- **EU hospitality workforce:** ~10.6M (Western/Northern/Southern Europe, Q1 2025)
- **UK hospitality workforce:** ~1.65M (ONS provisional)
- **US leisure and hospitality:** ~17M jobs (2024)
- **Annual turnover:** 70–80% (US BLS — highest of any sector); structurally stable, not cyclical
- **Cost per departure:** >$5,000 in recruiting, onboarding, and lost productivity (OysterLink 2025, citing Fountain)
- **For a 20-location group with 70% annual turnover and 30 staff per location:** ~420 hires per year at >$2.1M in turnover-related cost. An ATS that reduces cost-per-hire by 20% has a clear ROI case.

## What This Product Is Not

- **Not an enterprise WFM platform:** Lapam-ATS is an ATS only. It does not do scheduling, time and attendance, or payroll. It hands off cleanly to Deputy, 7shifts, Sage, Xero — whatever the operator already uses.
- **Not a single-site tool:** Built for multi-location groups (5–50 locations). Single-site independents are not the ICP.
- **Not a generic ATS with hospitality branding:** Seasonal talent pool CRM, mobile-first no-login application, and UK right-to-work workflow are hospitality-native depth, not cosmetic overlays.
- **Not a US-native tool with EU aspirations:** UK/EU compliance architecture from day one. GDPR, UK right-to-work, EU AI Act-ready. US market is a roadmap item (Could-5), not launch geography.
