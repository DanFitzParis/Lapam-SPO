# Competitor Analysis: Hospitality ATS

## Research Frame

The core job hospitality operators hire an ATS to do:

> **"Help me fill frontline positions quickly enough to prevent service gaps, without creating admin overhead for already-stretched managers, across multiple locations, amid constant churn."**

This file assesses each competitor against seven job steps:

| # | Job Step | Description |
|---|----------|-------------|
| S1 | Source | Attract qualified applicants where they already look |
| S2 | Screen | Filter unsuitable candidates without reading every application |
| S3 | Communicate | Reach candidates fast before they take another job |
| S4 | Schedule | Arrange interviews and trial shifts without phone tag |
| S5 | Hire | Make offer, collect documents, verify right-to-work/eligibility |
| S6 | Onboard | Get them productive quickly; hand off to scheduling and payroll |
| S7 | Pipeline | Track candidate flow across multiple locations at a glance |

Rating key: ✅ Strong | ⚠️ Partial | ❌ Weak or absent

## Competitors

### 1. Harri

**Type:** Hospitality-native WFM platform with integrated ATS  
**Geography:** US, UK, Europe (54+ countries supported)  
**Target segment:** Multi-location hospitality groups, 100+ staff  
**URL:** harri.com

**Overview**

Harri is the most feature-complete hospitality-native workforce management platform in the market. It covers the full employee lifecycle: recruitment, scheduling, time tracking, communication, HR, and payroll (UK only). The ATS module is embedded within the broader platform rather than sold standalone.

**Pricing**

Custom/enterprise. Based on third-party quote data (Connecteam review, Jan 2026): approximately $875/month for 100 employees across 10 locations (Employee Experience tier). Implementation fee ~$2,800. Per-employee charges apply above headcount threshold. No free plan, no free trial. Add-ons (Harri IQ analytics module): ~$375/month additional. Pricing reported as "eye-watering" for teams under 100.

**Features**

- Multi-location job posting and candidate pipeline management
- ATS with application tracking and candidate sharing across estate locations
- Forecast-based scheduling with labor cost visibility
- Time and attendance, shift management
- In-app messaging (team and candidate)
- Payroll module (UK only — not available for US or global deployments)
- Harri IQ: business intelligence and custom analytics module
- 5 separate mobile apps (Harri Live: 4.7–4.8 stars on app stores)

| Step | Rating | Notes |
|------|--------|-------|
| S1 Source | ✅ | Multi-board posting; candidate sharing across estate |
| S2 Screen | ⚠️ | Basic filtering; no documented AI screening |
| S3 Communicate | ✅ | In-app messaging; candidate comms centralised |
| S4 Schedule | ✅ | Integrated with WFM; trial shift scheduling natural |
| S5 Hire | ⚠️ | Offer management present; right-to-work support limited outside UK |
| S6 Onboard | ⚠️ | Payroll integration UK only; global handoff fragmented |
| S7 Pipeline | ✅ | Multi-location visibility is the product's core strength |

**Strengths**
- Strongest multi-location pipeline visibility in the market
- Candidate sharing across estate (significant differentiator)
- Forecast-based scheduling tightly integrated with hiring
- Best-in-class customer support (consistently rated 10/10)
- Deep hospitality domain knowledge embedded in product design

**Weaknesses / Pain Signals (Grade A — user reviews)**
- Prohibitively expensive for businesses under ~100 staff
- UK-only payroll: global and US operators cannot run payroll natively
- Very limited third-party payroll integrations even in US market
- 5 separate mobile apps — fragmented and reported as glitchy
- Steep learning curve; complex initial setup
- Reviews note: "It's a shambles! Nothing works how it's supposed to" (Capterra); "glitchy at times, which may hinder the overall experience" (G2)
- Sold as bundled platform — cannot purchase ATS standalone at transparent pricing

**Competitive gap for Lapam-ATS:** Price-exclusivity ($875+/month) creates a large underserved market below the enterprise tier. UK-only payroll is a structural gap for any global operator. The 5-app fragmentation and glitchiness signal platform integration debt.

### 2. Hireology

**Type:** Multi-location ATS for select verticals (hospitality, automotive, healthcare, fitness)  
**Geography:** US-primary  
**Target segment:** Multi-location operators, franchise groups  
**URL:** hireology.com

**Overview**

Hireology was named #1 hotel ATS in the 2024 HotelTechAwards and placed in the top 10 across 200+ G2 Winter 2024 reports. It focuses on the hiring funnel — sourcing to onboarding — without deep WFM integration. Strong multi-location management and candidate communication automation.

**Pricing**

Custom (Essentials and Professional tiers). No public pricing. Targeted at mid-market multi-location operators.

**Features**

- Multi-channel job distribution to 50+ job boards (Indeed, LinkedIn, Facebook, Google, Glassdoor)
- Pre-screen surveys with automated filtering of unqualified candidates
- Unlimited SMS and email candidate communication with branded templates
- Calendar-synced self-scheduling for candidates (Google, Microsoft, Apple)
- Interview guides with scoring rubrics (legal compliance focus)
- Skills testing and verification (role-specific 15-minute assessments)
- Background checks and drug screenings
- Digital offer letters, e-signatures, document collection
- Onboarding workflow with HRIS/payroll data handoff
- Multi-location Action Center: all hiring activity on one screen
- Candidate tagging, scoring, pool management for future reqs
- Analytics: time-to-fill, cost-per-hire, source effectiveness

**Strengths**
- Strongest feature set in the hospitality ATS segment (HotelTechReport #1)
- Two-way SMS is a genuine differentiator for hourly candidate engagement
- Legal-compliance framing in interview guides reduces employer liability
- Built for multi-location from the ground up (not a retrofit)
- Strong analytics with source attribution

**Weaknesses / Pain Signals (Grade B — analyst reviews)**
- No built-in WFM/scheduling — requires external integration
- Custom pricing with no transparency — difficult to evaluate without sales engagement
- Reported UI friction and limitations in advanced customization (G2/Capterra patterns)
- US-primary; international compliance support is not documented
- Not a standalone hospitality product — same product sold to automotive and healthcare

**Competitive gap for Lapam-ATS:** No WFM integration and no transparent pricing. Largely US-focused. The multi-vertical positioning means it lacks hospitality-specific workflow logic (e.g., trial shifts, kitchen skills flags, seasonal capacity planning).

- Applicant communications workflow
- UK ATS with hospitality-specific features (active development)
- Labour forecasting tightly coupled with headcount planning
- PEO/HR compliance wrapper (demonstrated in Radical Hospitality Group deployment)

### 4. Workstream

**Type:** Mobile-first hourly hiring platform (ATS + HR + payroll)  
**Geography:** US-primary  
**Target segment:** Restaurant chains, retail, hourly multi-location operators  
**URL:** workstream.us

**Overview**

Workstream was built specifically for the speed problem in hourly hiring. Founded 2016 in San Francisco. Core thesis: hospitaliy/retail managers lose candidates because the process is too slow and too desktop-centric. Applications complete in under 3 minutes on mobile. Time-to-hire typically 3–5 days vs 2–3 weeks with traditional ATS. Notable clients: White Castle, Panda Express, Planet Fitness.

**Pricing**

Location-based:
- Essential: $109/location/month
- Professional: $199/location/month
- Enterprise: $399/location/month

**Features**

- Mobile-first application: under 3 minutes, no account creation required
- Text-based candidate communication (primary channel)
- Automated screening questions and workflow routing
- Interview scheduling automation
- QR code apply (in-store/on-premise recruitment)
- Virtual hiring events
- Shift scheduling integration
- Employee referral tracking
- HR and payroll modules (added more recently)
- AI assistant for payroll reconciliation

**Strengths**
- Best mobile application completion rate in the market (40–60% higher than traditional)
- Text-first communication matches how hourly workers actually communicate
- Price transparency (rare in this segment — clear location-based tiers)

- QR code in-venue application is operationally clever

**Weaknesses / Pain Signals (Grade A/B)**

- Limited reporting and analytics ("basic" per multiple reviews)

- Limited integration ecosystem vs enterprise ATS
- HR and payroll modules are newer additions, not core competency

**Competitive gap for Lapam-ATS:** US-only. No credible international compliance story. Pipeline visibility and analytics are weak. A product that matches Workstream's candidate speed with a proper multi-location dashboard and global compliance would be differentiated.

## Non-Obvious Alternatives

These are the tools and processes operators currently "hire" to do the same job when no purpose-built ATS is in place.

### 10. Culinary Agents / Poached Jobs

**Type:** Hospitality-specific job boards (no ATS functionality)  
**Geography:** US-primary  
**What job they do:** S1 (Source only)

Culinary Agents and Poached Jobs are the dominant hospitality-specific job boards in the US. Pricing: Culinary Agents ~$69/month per posting; Poached from ~$59/month. Neither provides pipeline management, screening, communication, or onboarding. Operators post here and then manage the inbound separately — usually via email or spreadsheet. The core limitation: visibility without workflow.

**JTBD gap:** Strong at sourcing but exit immediately at S1. Managers who use these without an ATS face the highest administrative load.

### 11. Spreadsheets + WhatsApp/Email (Dominant informal stack)

**Geography:** Global  
**What job they do:** S1–S7 (badly)

The most common "tool" in use across independent restaurants, seasonal hotel properties, and small chains globally. The Chef Network (UK) published a formal spreadsheet template in Sept 2025 because so many operators had no other system. Key patterns documented:

- Managers manually post to Indeed and window signs
- Applications arrive via email or paper; sorted in inboxes
- WhatsApp used for candidate communication (SmartRecruiters identified this as dominant channel for hospitality candidates, April 2025)
- Scheduling organised via phone calls and texts
- Candidates are lost in the shuffle regularly — managers "ghost" candidates unintentionally
- No cross-location visibility; each location runs independently
- WOTC, right-to-work checks done manually if at all

**JTBD gap:** Fails at every stage under any meaningful volume. The friction and lost candidates are the primary pain that ATS vendors are positioned to solve.

**Why this matters:** This is the current state for a large proportion of the mid-market target segment. Any ATS positioned against this alternative has a low bar to clear on functionality — but must be significantly simpler than enterprise tools to win the switch.

### 12. Staffing Agencies

**Geography:** Global  
**What job they do:** S1–S6 (outsourced, high cost, low control)

Staffing agencies are heavily used for seasonal peak coverage in hospitality — particularly hotels and large events venues. They handle sourcing, basic vetting, and payroll. Key limitations: expensive (typically 15–25% markup on hourly wages), no employer brand control, inconsistent candidate quality, and no pipeline learning — the same sourcing work repeats every season with no institutional knowledge retained. Operators lose visibility into who applied and why.

**JTBD gap:** Effective at volume coverage but at high cost and zero pipeline ownership. An ATS with strong seasonal talent pool management (like Eploy's CRM model) directly displaces a portion of agency spend by allowing operators to re-engage last season's good hires.

## Market-Level Observations

**Where the market clusters:**
1. **Enterprise/large-group tier** (Harri, Fourth, Fountain): Full-featured, expensive, complex. Require significant implementation effort. Pricing excludes operators under ~100 employees.
2. **Mid-market multi-location** (Hireology, CareerPlug, Eploy): Good ATS, partial WFM, limited international, often US/UK-only.
3. **SMB/single-site** (StaffedUp, Workstream, HigherMe): Speed and simplicity. Affordable. US-only. No meaningful analytics or multi-location management.
4. **Informal stack** (spreadsheets + WhatsApp + job boards): The default for a significant portion of the market.

**The unoccupied position:**

No product currently occupies the intersection of:
- Multi-location pipeline visibility (S7)
- Mobile-first candidate experience (S3, S4)
- Global compliance coverage (EU/UK/US) (S5)
- Transparent, accessible pricing (not enterprise-locked)
- Seasonal talent pool / re-engagement (S1)
- Lightweight WFM handoff (S6)

This is the gap that a hospitality-native ATS with global positioning could credibly occupy.
