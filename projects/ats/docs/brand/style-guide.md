# Lapam Brand Style Guide

**Purpose:** Visual identity reference for all Lapam marketing materials and product interfaces.
**Audience:** AI agents producing marketing deliverables, landing pages, pitch decks, and press materials.

---

## 1. Brand Identity

**Company name:** Write as **Lapam**, not LAPAM.
**Tagline style:** "Intelligent Hospitality"
**Tone:** People-centric, tech-powered, experienced. Relaxed, human, clear. Confident and practical, not glossy or overblown. Prefer active voice, contractions where natural, short paragraphs.

---

## 2. Typography

**Primary font:** Montserrat (Google Fonts)
**Minimum weight:** 400 (never use lighter)
**Heading pattern (marketing surfaces):** Bold Montserrat for primary text + lighter-weight italic for the accent word. Example: "What we **do**" where "do" is italic, lighter purple (#6746CA or similar).
**Size jumps:** Use 3x+ jumps between heading and body, not 1.5x.
**Weight extremes:** Use 300/400 vs 700/900, not midrange pairs.
**Text alignment:** Left-aligned by default. Centred for hero headings and section titles on marketing pages.

**Do not use:** Inter, Roboto, Open Sans, Lato, Arial, or system fonts.

```
neutral-600:  #23252E   (darkest — shell, footer)
neutral-500:  #2D2F3A   (primary text)
neutral-400:  #3D424F   (secondary text)
neutral-300:  #585F7B   (muted text)
neutral-200:  #9BA2C0   (soft text, disabled)
neutral-100:  #DADBE7   (borders, dividers)
neutral-50:   #F3F2F5   (app/page background)
neutral-0:    #FFFFFF   (cards, panels)
```

### 3.2 Brand Purple (primary palette)

```
brand-400:    #1D122C   (deepest purple — dark sections, footer, shell)
brand-300:    #301E4A   (primary action, buttons, dark backgrounds)
brand-200:    #D6D3FF   (light purple — tints, badges, soft backgrounds)
brand-100:    #F2EEF9   (lightest purple — section backgrounds, subtle tints)
```

### 3.3 Brand Accent (teal)

```
accent:       #18B6C3   (brand teal — decorative only)
```

**Rules:** Use teal sparingly for decorative accents, progress indicators, icon highlights, and brand signals. **Never** use teal as primary button colour, main text colour, or solid action background.

## 4. Marketing Surface Patterns

These patterns are extracted from the brand's public website and presentation decks. Use them for landing pages, pitch decks, and marketing collateral.

### 4.1 Hero Section

- Large bold Montserrat heading (48–64px equivalent)
- Accent word in italic, lighter purple weight
- Subheading in neutral-400, regular weight, 18–20px
- Primary CTA: solid purple pill button
- Secondary CTA: outlined purple pill button
- Right side: product screenshots in circular or rounded-rectangle frames, with warm hospitality photography
- Background: white or very light lavender (#F2EEF9) with subtle purple gradient accent in upper right

### 4.2 Section Labels (Pills)

- Small rounded-border pill above section headings
- Light border, small text (12–14px), neutral colour
- Example: `Our solutions`, `Key features`, `Challenges`
- Used consistently to introduce every major page section

### 4.3 Section Headings

- Pattern: "Bold text *italic accent*"
- Example: "What makes us *different*"
- Bold portion: neutral-500 or neutral-600
- Italic accent: brand-300 or #6746CA (lighter purple)
- Centred on full-width sections, left-aligned on split layouts

### 4.4 Feature Cards

- White background
- Subtle border (neutral-100) or borderless with light shadow
- Rounded corners (12–16px radius)
- Top: icon in light purple square (brand-100 background, brand-300 icon)
- Bold title, then descriptive text in neutral-400
- Optional "Learn more" link in brand-300

### 4.5 Stats/Metrics Bar

- Dark background (brand-400 #1D122C) or purple gradient
- Large bold numbers in white or accent teal
- Small label text below each number in white/soft opacity
- Icon badges alongside each metric
- Full-width band, creates visual break between sections

### 4.6 Testimonial/Social Proof Section

- Light grey background (#F3F2F5)
- Cards with subtle borders, large quotation marks as decorative element
- Customer quote in regular weight
- Attribution: bold name, regular role/company
- Logo row: greyscale client logos on white, evenly spaced

### 4.7 Dark Sections

- Background: brand-400 (#1D122C) or deep purple gradient
- Text: white headings, soft white (rgba 255,255,255,0.88) body
- Used for: platform showcase sections, stats bars, CTA sections, footer
- Geometric shapes: half-circles, quarter-circles, overlapping rounds in brand-200/brand-300 as decorative elements

### 4.8 Photography Style

- Warm hospitality scenes: restaurants, hotels, kitchens, service staff
- Neon teal/purple lighting accents in the photography
- Circular or rounded-rectangle frame crops
- Used in hero sections, feature showcases, and testimonial areas
- **For agent-generated pages:** Use CSS gradient placeholders or coloured divs where photos would go. Describe what image would appear (e.g. "Hospitality team member using tablet in restaurant setting")

### 4.9 Footer

- Dark background (brand-400 #1D122C)
- White text, organised in multi-column layout
- Product links, company links, resource links
- Logo in white/inverse treatment
- Copyright line at bottom

### 4.10 CTA Buttons

- Primary: solid brand-300 background, white text, pill radius (fully rounded)
- Secondary: white/transparent background, brand-300 border + text, pill radius
- Tertiary: text link in brand-300, no background
- Hover: darken to brand-400
- Mobile: full-width buttons in key positions

## 5. Pitch Deck Patterns

From the corporate and board presentation decks:

### 5.1 Section Dividers

- Full-bleed dark purple (brand-400) background
- Large bold section number in very heavy weight (900), semi-transparent
- Section title in white bold + accent word in italic brand-200
- Geometric shapes (half-circles, quarter-circles) in brand-300 and brand-200 on right side

### 5.2 Content Slides

- White or light lavender background
- Left: text content with bold heading, body in neutral-400
- Right: product screenshot, diagram, or hospitality photography
- Icon bullets using brand purple icon squares
- Data/metrics in large bold brand-300 numbers

### 5.3 Process/Flow Slides

- Numbered steps (01, 02, 03) in italic brand-300
- Step cards: light background with rounded corners
- Arrow connectors between steps in brand-200
- Clean, spacious layout

---

## 6. Tone of Voice (Marketing)

- Write for hospitality operators and hiring managers
- Be direct, warm, and practical
- Lead with the customer's problem, not the product
- Use "you/your" language, not "we/our" in headlines
- Prefer specific outcomes over vague promises
- Avoid: "revolutionary", "game-changing", "simple", "easy", "seamless" (overused)
- Prefer: concrete verbs, outcome language, industry-specific terms
- UK English spelling (colour, optimise, centralise)

---

## 7. Anti-Patterns

Do not:
- Use brand teal as primary button or text colour
- Use Inter, Roboto, Arial, or generic system fonts
- Default to white-and-purple gradients on solid white backgrounds (the AI slop aesthetic)
- Flood pages with photography — use strategically in hero and showcase sections
- Mix icon families
- Use generic bright SaaS blue
- Overcrowd layouts — maintain generous white space
- Use Montserrat below weight 400
