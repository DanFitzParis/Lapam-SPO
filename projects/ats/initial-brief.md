# Research Brief: Lapam-ATS

## Client

**Codename:** Lapam

**What agents know:** Lapam is a hospitality management software vendor operating across 54 countries.

**Information boundaries:** Clean-room. All research must be from public sources only. No reference to any acquisition target, internal intelligence, or the client's existing product portfolio.

## Mission

**Domain:** Applicant tracking systems for the hospitality sector

**Strategic question:** Is there a defensible opportunity for a hospitality-native ATS, and what would it take to build one?

**Context:** Client has identified applicant tracking as a potential portfolio addition. Research informs a build-vs-buy-vs-partner decision. This is a zero-to-one experiment — there are no existing customers, no usage data, no feedback loops. All conclusions are hypotheses, not validated findings.

## Scope

**Stage:** Zero-to-one

**Geographic focus:** Global, emphasis EU/UK/US

**Output files:** standard-7

## Guidance

- Multi-location, high-turnover environments are the primary pain point to investigate
- Greenfield build assumption — research should surface what a purpose-built solution would need
- Volume profile: high volume, low complexity, extreme seasonality
- JTBD framing required for competitor analysis — assess which job steps each competitor serves well and where they fall short
- Include non-obvious alternatives (manual processes, WhatsApp, spreadsheets, staffing agencies) as current "hires" for the same job

## Success Criteria

- Every major claim traces to a source in the evidence ledger
- Low-confidence conclusions are clearly flagged
- The executive summary supports a preliminary build-vs-buy-vs-partner decision
- Competitor analysis uses JTBD framing, not just feature lists
- Domain-specific dynamics are surfaced, not just generic analysis
- Regulatory and compliance constraints treated as first-class outputs (EU/UK employment law, GDPR, US EEOC, right-to-work verification)
